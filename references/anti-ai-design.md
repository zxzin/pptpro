# PPTPRO Anti-AI Design Gate

Use this reference for every new deck and every substantial redesign. It converts anti-AI taste into observable PowerPoint decisions rather than a vague request to make slides look better.

## Contents

- Direction lock
- Design passes
- Composition rules
- Typography and copy
- Color and material
- Cards, diagrams, and decoration
- Images and evidence
- AI-slop test
- Render review

## Direction Lock

Before choosing colors or slide recipes, write one sentence that identifies:

- who will see the deck
- where and how it will be viewed
- what the audience must understand or decide
- which source, object, result, or idea should carry the visual identity

Then choose one design direction. Do not average several aesthetics together.

Precedence:

1. Supplied template or explicit brand system.
2. Explicit visual direction from the user.
3. Zinx mode when named.
4. PPTPRO editorial default.

Anti-AI discipline applies in every mode. Template fidelity can justify a normally discouraged device, but only when it is an established part of the supplied system.

## Design Passes

Run these passes in order. Skip a pass only when the supplied template already resolves it.

1. **Distill:** remove duplicate claims, filler labels, unnecessary cards, and details that belong in the speaker script.
2. **Layout:** establish one focal point, meaningful grouping, alignment, density, and a clear reading path.
3. **Typeset:** set title rhythm, scale contrast, line length, label hierarchy, and readable body size before decorating.
4. **Colorize:** assign semantic color roles and verify contrast; do not spread accent color uniformly across every object.
5. **Calibrate:** make a bland slide bolder through scale, composition, imagery, or commitment; make a noisy slide quieter by removing competing accents and containers.
6. **Polish:** inspect edge alignment, optical centering, crop quality, spacing consistency, text fit, and sequence rhythm in rendered previews.

When a slide still feels generic after these passes, change its composition or source object. Do not solve it with more decoration.

## Composition Rules

- Give every slide one dominant message and one dominant visual relationship.
- Make the source, figure, object, comparison, process, or decision visible. Do not hide the subject behind generic containers.
- Vary composition by meaning. A sequence may deliberately repeat; unrelated slides must not share the same title + three cards + bottom bar scaffold by reflex.
- Use whitespace to frame content. Reject both large unexplained holes and dense edge-to-edge packing.
- Keep visual weight through the middle and lower-middle canvas instead of stacking everything under the title.
- Use asymmetry when it improves hierarchy. Do not default to rigid 50/50 columns.
- Use cards only for discrete repeated units, comparisons, evidence, or controls. Never put cards inside cards.
- Match container size to content. A short sentence does not need a giant panel.
- Prefer a designed visual system over a pile of individually polished elements.

## Typography And Copy

- Use a type scale with clear roles: display title, slide title, body, annotation, source, and HUD/label when relevant.
- Keep body text readable at talk scale. Move nuance into the speaker script.
- Use conclusion-style titles when the slide presents evidence; avoid generic labels such as `Overview`, `Results`, `Key Insights`, or `Our Approach` unless they are structurally necessary.
- Use direct claims, concrete nouns, and named defects. Avoid motivational filler and inflated transitions.
- Do not use tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows on every slide.
- Do not use `01 / 02 / 03` as default section decoration. Numbers must communicate a real sequence, level, count, or order.
- Do not expose workflow language such as `source log`, `final audit`, `working notes`, or `provenance recorded` in audience-facing material.

For Chinese visible copy and speaker scripts, reject formulaic contrast and self-media filler unless quoting or critiquing it:

- `不是 A，而是 B`
- `不是 A，是 B`
- `真正的问题是`
- `真正的问题变成了`
- `与其说 A，不如说 B`
- `表面上看是 A，实际上是 B`
- `换句话说`
- `值得注意的是`
- `不难发现`
- `归根结底`
- `一句话讲清楚`
- `很多人忽略了`
- `这才是关键`
- `形成记忆点`
- `打造差异化`
- `接住`

Rewrite as a direct claim, causal statement, concrete action, named defect, or scene label.

## Color And Material

- Choose a color strategy before individual colors: restrained, committed, full palette, or drenched.
- Do not infer palette from category alone. Finance does not automatically mean navy and gold; AI does not automatically mean purple-blue gradients; education does not automatically mean beige editorial paper.
- Avoid beige, cream, sand, parchment, and warm-paper backgrounds as an unrequested default.
- Avoid one-note palettes where every surface is a variation of the same hue.
- Use gray text carefully on colored surfaces; use a darker shade of the surface hue or a strong neutral with sufficient contrast.
- Verify contrast in the rendered slide, not only in theme tokens.
- Liquid glass, glow, grain, gradients, and particles are materials, not proof of design. Use them only when the selected direction gives them a role.

## Cards, Diagrams, And Decoration

Reject these as default scaffolding:

- identical icon + heading + paragraph card grids
- hero metric templates with one large number and supporting mini-stats
- colored side-stripe borders on cards and callouts
- gradient text
- decorative grid, stripe, scanline, or blueprint backgrounds
- unexplained short dashes, glowing connector lines, corner rails, and full-width light dividers
- thick border plus large soft shadow on the same element
- oversized rounded rectangles or pill-shaped content panels
- random icons used instead of a real source object, figure, image, or diagram
- the same entrance/reveal treatment implied on every slide

Every line must connect, divide, measure, point, or encode. Every box must group a real unit. Every accent must establish hierarchy, state, or meaning.

## Images And Evidence

- Use real source visuals, original figures, official screenshots, maps, objects, and contextual images before decorative illustration.
- Keep faces visible when portrait imagery is relevant. Crop lower body, background, or side margins before cutting off a face.
- Preserve the meaning, aspect ratio, labels, axes, legends, and context of evidence images.
- Reject vague stock imagery, fake dashboards, decorative laptop mockups, low-resolution assets, and generated filler.
- Generated images may support a concept or scene, but they cannot replace factual evidence, official interfaces, real people, source figures, or documents.
- Do not place an image simply because a layout has an empty slot. Redesign the layout when the image has no argumentative role.

## AI-Slop Test

Run this test at two levels before handoff:

1. **Category reflex:** Could the palette and layout have been predicted from the topic alone? If yes, return to the direction lock.
2. **Anti-reference reflex:** Could the design have been predicted from the topic plus a request to avoid the obvious style? If yes, the deck has only switched to a second generic aesthetic.

Also ask:

- Can three consecutive slides be mistaken for template duplicates?
- Is the actual subject visible, or only cards and labels?
- Does the deck contain a visual decision that is specific to this source and audience?
- Is any effect compensating for weak hierarchy?
- Could the visible copy have been generated for almost any topic?

Any `yes` requires redesign, not decorative polish.

## Render Review

Inspect every rendered slide at normal viewing size, then review the sequence as thumbnails.

- At normal size, verify hierarchy, contrast, text fit, image crops, and source readability.
- In thumbnail view, verify pacing, varied composition, section rhythm, and palette control.
- Check the cover, densest slide, sparsest slide, every portrait slide, every original data figure, and the closing slide separately.
- Remove artifacts that attract attention without communicating information.
- Rerender after repair. Do not approve a deck from source code or package structure alone.
